Alison Casbourne

classical mezzo-soprano

Alison is a classically trained mezzo-soprano who specialises in opera and sacred music, bringing a fresh perspective to well known and loved classical repertoire. Based in the Midlands, Alison is a member of OperaMint, and is mentored by opera singer and teacher Sara Clethero (Welsh National Opera).


She began her vocal training in Hong Kong under the direction of Dr Derek Anthony singing with the Opera Society of Hong Kong, the Hong Kong Academy for Performing Arts and the St John’s Cathedral Choir in Verdi’s ‘La Traviata’ and Mozart’s ‘Magic Flute’.

In recent years Alison sang as part of a classical vocal duo with soprano Jill Saunders, performing in numerous recitals, concerts, military and charity events. Highlights included being guest artists in the Gentlemen Songsters Male Voice Choir Autumn Concert, 2012, which featured the well known Welsh baritone and TV personality, Aled Jones. 


She has also formed before HRH The Countess of Wessex GCVO during a QARANC Corps Day Service and recently performed in Lichfield Cathedral at a celebration of Whittington Barracks receiving the Freedom of the City. Alison continues to sing regularly in OperaMint concerts recently performing in Kaltern, Italy with the company. 


Alison is currently studying the role of Dorabella in Mozart's opera 'Cosi fan tutte' to be performed as part of a summer school programme in The Musicale, Kaltern, July 2017. She will also be performing in a recital and other concerts as part of this event. 


Alison is passionate about bringing classical music to the mainstream so that it is accessible to all. She sings with a voice of true expression, character and emotion and always aims to leave her audiences wanting more.
